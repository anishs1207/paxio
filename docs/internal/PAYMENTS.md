Why one-time payments are better for new startups
1️⃣ Higher conversion (especially in India 🇮🇳)

Users hesitate with auto-debit / UPI mandate

Many drop off when asked for recurring permission

One-time “Pay ₹X for 30 days” feels safer

2️⃣ Much faster to ship

Razorpay Orders API is dead simple

No mandate setup

No retry / cancellation / edge-case logic

You can launch in 1–2 days

3️⃣ Less support + legal headache

No “why was I charged again?” tickets

Fewer chargebacks

No RBI / mandate-related friction

4️⃣ You’re still validating the product

Early stage goal =
“Will people pay at all?”
Not “optimize MRR yet”.


What MOST successful Indian startups do (real pattern)
Phase 1: MVP (0 → first 1000 users)

✅ One-time payment
✅ Time-based access (30 / 90 days)
✅ Manual renewal

Example:
“₹299 – Access for 30 days”

Phase 2: Traction (people coming back)

➕ Add monthly subscription
➕ Keep one-time plans alive

Let users choose.

Phase 3: Scale

🔥 Push subscriptions as default
🔥 Annual plans with discount
🔥 One-time becomes secondary
